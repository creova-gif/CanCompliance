Tier 2 — Product features for public adoption
Features that drive mass uptake
Research shows 35% of regulations are pure red tape — unnecessary burden with no public interest value. The public adoption flywheel is built on making compliance feel like an advantage, not a tax. These features create network effects, habit formation, and defensible switching costs.
Product · Feature 5
Compliance health network (B2B2B)
When a business shares their compliance certificate, their suppliers see it. Build a "Request compliance proof from supplier" feature — a business can request that a vendor prove their CASL, PIPEDA, or S-211 status. Each request pulls a new user into the network. Network effects: every certified business becomes a distribution node for the next customer.

How to earn government endorsement — in sequence
Now
Phase 0
Build the red tape burden calculator + launch publicly
Low effort, high government visibility. CFIB will cover it. Media will write about it. It costs $0 and positions you as a public-interest tool before you have 100 users.
Month 3
Phase 1
Submit to ISED's BizPaL and Centre for Regulatory Innovation
Apply for the Centre for Regulatory Innovation's partnership program. Contact BizPaL's national office at ISED. Request to be listed as a preferred RegTech tool. These are open programs — no political connections needed.
Month 6
Phase 2
Publish the first Canadian SMB Compliance Benchmarking Report
Anonymized aggregate data from your user base. Send to CFIB, Statistics Canada, the Treasury Board Secretariat, and every provincial chamber of commerce. This is the moment you become a policy-relevant data source — not just a SaaS tool.
Month 9
Phase 3
Pitch provincial governments as a preferred SMB compliance tool
Ontario's Red Tape Challenge, Alberta's SMART Regulation initiative, and BC's Better Regulation Office all have procurement pathways for RegTech. A provincial "endorsed tool" badge is worth 10x more than any ad spend for SMB adoption.
Year 2
Phase 4
Regulatory Sandbox partnership with OSFI / FCAC / CRTC
Apply to pilot the Digital Regulatory Reporting (DRR) pipeline with a single regulator — CRTC for CASL is the easiest first. Once one regulator accepts structured data from CanCompliance, you become part of Canada's compliance infrastructure. That is the moat no competitor can replicate.
The one-sentence positioning shift these features enable
CanCompliance stops being "a compliance tool for SMBs" and becomes "Canada's compliance infrastructure layer" — the platform that governments cite in policy papers, lawyers recommend to clients, accountants use to serve their books, and developers embed in their products. That is a different category of business, with a different multiple, and a different YC conversation.
